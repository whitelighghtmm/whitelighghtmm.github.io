Parent Training


Parental Involvement Matters!

Research has shown that parents who are actively involved with their children’s therapy will see better outcomes at the end of their early intervention program. The reasons for this can be manifold. Active parents are more likely to want to learn how to communicate more effectively with their child and to understand their child’s cues for interaction.

Parents who understand how therapy is carried out and how they can implement the program are also effectively extending the hours of their therapy. Even if they do not act as supplementary therapists, they are strengthening the program by supporting it and using similar intervention strategies during their interactions with their children.

We understand the frustrations and parenting challenges that you may experience due to the obstacles brought about by developmental delays. You’re not alone. Please get in touch with us, so we can find ways to support you and your family in this journey.

Complimentary Parent Observation


For parents with their child in our therapy program, you are encouraged to observe your child’s session. Guided by the therapist, you will gain a better appreciation for how your child’s Individualized Education Plan (IEP) is being carried out and the ways you can support or assist your therapist to help your child learn.

We understand that it may not be possible for all parents to sit in for therapy due to the demands of work, so we also offer some other options below for parent education.

1:1 Parent Training


For parents who wish to learn about ABA-VB but are not able to sit in on the therapy, or for parents who are serious about learning the ABA-VB methodology, we offer customized individual parent training.

This can be a short course to introduce you and your child’s caregivers to the fundamentals and basic principles of ABA-VB. It can also be an intensive course if you have the interest or the motivation to train in the techniques so that you can become your child’s therapist and supplement your child’s total weekly hours of therapy.

For overseas clients who may face difficulty recruiting therapists in your vicinity, you may wish to consider intensive training for yourself while your child is attending the LEAP program.

Group Seminars / Sessions


ARN also organizes group seminars or workshops for parents, which may be based around topics like language development or helping a child cope in mainstream school. You will get to meet other parents to share valuable insights and advice on how to support your child with special needs.

At other times, we may organize events like lectures or conferences, such as when Dr. Partington is in town. These are valuable opportunities for parents to learn from an expert in the field with over 45 years of hands-on experience pioneering breakthroughs in ABA therapy.
