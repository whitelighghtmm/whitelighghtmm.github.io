School Integration Support


Starting school is a major milestone in every child’s life and helping your child with special needs successfully into schooling is one of our top priorities. Whether your child is enrolling in a mainstream school or a school with SEN (Special Education Needs) support, we are here to help every step of the way.

We consult with the administrative staff of mainstream schools to assist them in accommodating the needs of the child and to ensure the smooth transition of the child into their care.

These consultation sessions are intended to identify the best ways to support the child within the school. Our consultants will collaborate with school personnel in designing a customized intervention and teaching plan that meets the child’s educational needs and fulfills curriculum objectives. This plan may include school shadowing, which is explained further below.

Initial meetings will usually cover:

• Each child’s capabilities and weaknesses in a classroom setting
• Creating an individualized plan to tackle the child’s needs
• Meeting with teachers and training them to be able to carry out the decided plan of action
• Regular updates and revisions of the plan based on the child’s progress.

School Shadowing


Enrollment in a mainstream school may be a milestone, but for your child, it is simply the beginning of the challenges he or she will face. Having a school shadowing plan can alleviate the stress and anxiety your child has in adapting to a new school environment and provide on-the-spot coaching for new and unfamiliar social situations.

A school shadow is a trained therapist who accompanies your child to school and remains with your child throughout the day. A school shadow can start off by actively helping but will usually fadetheir presence as your child becomes more independent, until they are no longer needed. When your child’s therapists shadow them in school, your child will experience less stress and anxiety than with a ‘sink or swim’ approach. They can coach your child how to self-regulate in the school environment, follow daily routines, and respond to social situations. (make font size consistent)

One common reason when behavioral problems arise in school after a successful transition can be that your child may not be coping well with the difficulty level of the curriculum. If your child is ‘acting out’, find out whether the school work is the source of stress. In addition to carrying out your child’s IEP, our therapists can help coach your child to complete his or her homework assignments. If needed, they can also provide tutoring, which can help lower your child’s stress level and improve self-regulation.
