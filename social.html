Social Thinking® Program

Your child with special needs may face difficulties adapting to new surroundings and interacting with others whether at school or in everyday life. While your child may have good language and cognitive abilities, knowing how to behave appropriately and interact well with peers might still be a tricky concept to grasp. At ARN, we use the award-winning Social Thinking® program, which goes beyond the teaching of social skills to show children how the social mind works and how social behavior influences the way people perceive them.

Often, we expect children to demonstrate social skills such as sharing feelings, taking turns and staying on topic without addressing a very important fact:

Social cognition is not simply a set of skills, but a social learning process.

About Social Thinking®

Social Thinking® is a curriculum developed for children with special needs by acclaimed author and Speech Language Pathologist, Michelle Garcia Winner. It teaches your child to manage his or her behaviors for more rewarding social outcomes while considering how others like his or her school teachers and fellow classmates perceive and respond to them. It also helps them adapt to people and situations around them, from more structured settings during school lessons to more free-flow situations during recess.
